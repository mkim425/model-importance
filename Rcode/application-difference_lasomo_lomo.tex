\textbf{Overall Observations:}

\begin{enumerate}
\def\labelenumi{\arabic{enumi})}
\item
  The overall impression is that a model that already has good
  predictive performance as a standalone model can also make important
  contributions to both trained and untrained ensembles, helping the
  ensemble achieve higher accuracy.
\item
  The overall performance of the Karlen-pypm model looks good as the
  model's scores in all metrics are the best when replacing NAs with the
  worst score, despite the low submission rate. The strategy of
  replacing NAs with the average reflects how excellent the overall
  performance of the Karlen-pypm model is. The Karlen-pypm model has the
  best scores by far in all metrics.
\item
  Regardless of whether the ensembles were trained or not, the
  importance scores for models based on the all-possible-subsets-out
  algorithm (i.e., Shapley value) have similar trends as those based on
  the leave-one-model-out algorithm.
\item
  The slight differences in weighted ensemble based mean importance
  scores between the two approaches may be due to some randomness in the
  estimation of weights during the training process (e.g.~when handling
  missing values by replacing with the worst score, USC-SI\_kJalpha has
  a little lower score than COVIDhub-baseline in LOMO\_wt unlike
  Shapley\_wt).
\item
  Interestingly, the NA deletion strategy gives nearly the same score
  trend as replacing NAs with the average score.
\end{enumerate}
